The name means something

Your lawn doesn't need the same thing in July as it does in January. We plan around that.

Waikato grass and gardens shift a lot across the year — so our rounds do too. Here's roughly how the work changes, season to season.

Sep — Nov

Spring

Growth kicks back in fast. We ramp mowing up to weekly or fortnightly and clear out whatever winter left behind in the beds.

Dec — Feb

Summer

Peak season. Regular mowing, sharp edges, and hedge shaping while everything's putting on its fastest growth of the year.

Mar — May

Autumn

Leaf clearing, green waste removal, and a final hedge trim before things slow down for winter.

Jun — Aug

Winter

Slower growth means longer gaps between mows, so we shift focus to garden tidy-ups and getting beds ready for spring.
