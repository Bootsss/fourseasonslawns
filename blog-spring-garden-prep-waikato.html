Preparing Your Garden for Spring in the Waikato

Four Seasons Lawn Services Seasonal Guide 4 min read

Spring is when Waikato gardens properly wake up — and getting a few jobs done early makes the rest of the season much easier to manage. Here's a practical rundown of what's worth doing before the growth really kicks in.

Clear winter debris first

Fallen branches, matted leaves and soggy garden waste tend to build up over winter, especially in beds and along fence lines. Clearing this out early lets light and air back into garden beds and stops it smothering anything trying to grow underneath.

Get ahead of weeds

Weeds wake up just as fast as everything else in spring, and they're far easier to deal with when they're small. A thorough weed of garden beds in early spring saves a lot of work later in the season when both the weeds and the desirable plants are competing for the same growth spurt.

Check your mowing schedule

If your lawn was on a monthly winter round, it's worth reviewing that early — Waikato grass can go from barely growing to needing a mow every one to two weeks within a few short weeks of warmer weather arriving. Falling behind here means a harder, messier first cut.

Give hedges a shape-up

Late spring is a good window for the first proper hedge trim of the year, once the risk of hard frosts has passed and plants are actively growing again. This sets the shape for the rest of the season rather than trying to correct it later.

Look at drainage while it's fresh in mind

If any parts of your section held water through winter, spring — while it's still reasonably damp — is the easiest time to spot and address those low spots, before summer dries everything out and the problem becomes invisible again until next winter.

Don't feel like you have to do it all at once

Spring garden tidy-ups are one of the most common jobs we get asked to help with, precisely because trying to catch up on a whole winter's growth in one weekend is a big ask. A single clean-up visit to reset the garden, followed by a regular mowing and maintenance round, is usually the easiest way to stay ahead of it for the rest of the year.
