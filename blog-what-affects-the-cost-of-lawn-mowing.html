What Affects the Cost of Lawn Mowing in NZ?

Four Seasons Lawn Services Pricing Guide 4 min read

"How much does a mow cost?" is one of the first things almost everyone asks — and the honest answer is that it depends on a handful of factors that can shift the price more than people expect. Here's what actually goes into a quote.

Section size

This is the biggest factor. A small in-town lawn takes a fraction of the time of a large rural section or a lifestyle block, so pricing scales with area. It's also why "average" prices you see online can be misleading — they're usually based on a section size that may not match yours at all.

Access and terrain

A flat, open, easy-access lawn mows faster than one with steep banks, tight side accesses, lots of trees and garden beds to trim around, or gates that need opening and closing. Trickier terrain means more time on the mower and more hand-trimming, which affects price.

Growth rate and grass type

A lawn that's mowed regularly on a set schedule is quicker (and often cheaper per visit) than one that's grown out and needs a harder cut. Grass type matters too — some varieties grow faster and thicker than others and need more frequent attention through the warmer months.

How often you want it done

Regular scheduled mowing (weekly or fortnightly) is usually more cost-effective per visit than occasional one-off mows, since the lawn stays shorter and faster to cut. An overgrown lawn that's gotten away on you will typically cost more for that first "catch-up" mow.

What's included

Check whether a quote includes edging, trimming around obstacles, and clearing clippings from paths and driveways — some cheaper quotes only cover the mow itself and treat everything else as an extra. We include all of that as standard.

Commercial vs residential

Commercial and body corporate properties are often quoted differently to residential jobs, since they tend to involve larger areas, more obstacles (car parks, signage, garden islands), and sometimes scheduled invoicing rather than a pay-per-visit arrangement.
